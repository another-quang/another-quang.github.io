Live Minutes – House Chamber – 25 August 2015

09:00 AM
1

Commencement

The House met, at 9 am, pursuant to adjournment. Madam Speaker (the Honourable Bronwyn Bishop) took the Chair, made an acknowledgement of country and read Prayers.
09:01 AM
2

Mr Dutton: To present a Bill for an Act to amend the Ombudsman Act 1976, and for related purposes. (Private Health Insurance Amendment Bill (No. 2) 2014)

Message No. 149, 6 February 2015, from His Excellency the Governor-General was announced recommending, in accordance with the requirements of section 56 of the Constitution, an appropriation for the purposes of the Appropriation Bill (No. 3) 2014-2015.

Ms O’Dwyer (Parliamentary Secretary to the Treasurer) presented a Bill for an Act to appropriate additional money out of the Consolidated Revenue Fund for the ordinary annual services of the Government, and for related purposes.

Document

Ms O’Dwyer presented an explanatory memorandum to the bill.

Bill read a first time

Ms O’Dwyer moved—That the bill be now read a second time

Debate adjourned (Mr Clare), and the resumption of the debate made an order of the day for the next sitting.

09:04 AM
3

Mr Ciobo: To present a Bill for an Act to abolish the Corporations and Markets Advisory Committee, and for related purposes. (Australian Securities and Investments Commission Amendment (Corporations and Markets Advisory Committee Abolition) Bill 2014)

Message No. 150, 6 February 2015, from His Excellency the Governor-General was announced recommending, in accordance with the requirements of section 56 of the Constitution, an appropriation for the purposes of the Appropriation Bill (No. 4) 2014-2015.

Ms O’Dwyer (Parliamentary Secretary to the Treasurer) presented a Bill for an Act to appropriate additional money out of the Consolidated Revenue Fund for certain expenditure, and for related purposes.

Document

Bill read a first time.

Ms O’Dwyer moved—That the bill be now read a second time.

Debate adjourned (Mr Clare), and the resumption of the debate made an order of the day for the next sitting

09:09 AM
4

Fair Work Amendment (bargaining Processes) Bill 2014

Second reading—Resumption of debate (from 27 November 2014 — Mr Clare)

Mr Broadbent (Chair) presented the following document:

Report concerning an application from Mr Marcus Lee for the publication of a response to a reference made in the House of Representatives, February 2015.

On the motion of Mr Broadbent, by leave, the report was agreed to.

Mr Broadbent, by leave, made a statement in connection with the report.

09:19 AM
5

Mr Pyne: To move—That standing order 31 (automatic adjournment of the House) and standing order 33 (limit on business) be suspended for the sitting on Thursday, 4 December 2014.

The order of the day having been read for the resumption of the debate on the question—That the bill be now read a second time—

Debate resumed.

Ms L. M. Chesters, 10:47:03 AM. Mrs Prentice, 10:56:04 AM. Ms Ryan, 11:03:39 AM. Ms F. M. Scott, 11:18:48 AM. Mr Watts, 11:33:30 AM. Mr Hutchinson, 11:47:09 AM. Ms O’Neil, 12:02:15 PM. Mr Wyatt Roy, 12:17:47 PM.

Debate adjourned (Mrs K. L. Andrews—Parliamentary Secretary to the Minister for Industry and Science), and the resumption of the debate made an order of the day for a later hour this day.

10:01 AM
6

Social Services And Other Legislation Amendment (2014 Budget Measures No. 5) Bill 2014

Second reading—Resumption of debate (from 2 October 2014 — Mr A. S. Burke)
